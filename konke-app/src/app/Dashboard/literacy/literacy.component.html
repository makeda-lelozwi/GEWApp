Financial Literacy for Entrepreneurs

1. Separate Personal and Business Finances

Open a dedicated business account to track income and expenses separately from your personal finances. This helps maintain clarity and ensures proper tax compliance.

2. Create a Budget

Develop a detailed monthly budget that includes all expenses, savings, and investments. Review it regularly to avoid overspending.

3. Monitor Cash Flow

Keep a close eye on your cash flow. Ensure that more money is coming into the business than going out to maintain solvency and fund growth opportunities.

4. Reinvest in Your Business

Allocate a portion of your profits to growth initiatives, such as marketing, staff training, or purchasing better equipment to scale effectively.

5. Build an Emergency Fund

Save at least 3-6 months of operational costs in an emergency fund to prepare for unexpected challenges or slow periods.

6. Seek Professional Advice

Consult a financial advisor or accountant to ensure your business remains compliant and financially healthy.
